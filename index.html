Lemon Glazed Madeleines

Ingredients


  • 195 g sugar
  • 4 eggs
  • 36 g honey
  • 225 g butter
  • 2 tsp lemon zest
  • 225 g flour
  • 6 g baking powder

Glaze


  • 65 g fresh lemon juice
  • 225g icing sugar

Method


In a large heat-proof bowl, combine sugar, eggs and honey. Place the bowl over a gently simmering pot of water and whisk thoroughly until sugar is dissolved and mixture is smooth and heated to 35 C/95 F.

Meanwhile, melt butter to 35 C/95 F. Stir in lemon zest.

Fold dry ingredients into egg mixture in two additions. Fold in butter until smooth.

Transfer batter to a piping bag or container and refrigerate at least 4 hrs or overnight. At this point, batter can be kept in the fridge up to a week. (It can also be frozen for up to a month. Thaw in the fridge overnight before using.)

When youâ€™re ready to bake, chill madeleine pan in freezer 30 min. Preheat oven to 375 F.

Make your glaze: whisk lemon juice with icing sugar until smooth. Glaze will keep in the fridge up to 5 days.

Spray madeleine pan lightly with baking spray. Fill each cavity with 25 g batter (about 1/3 full). Bake until the edges are golden and crisp and domed tops are set, about 9 min (for a standard 12-cavity pan). Unmold cakes and set on a cooling rack. Glaze the bump side while cakes are warm. Madeleines are best eaten within a few hours of baking.
